## 📄 Preface

> "The ultimate frontier of perception algorithms is not infinite data, but the unyielding boundaries of physical laws."

This project is not a concrete code repository (yet). It is a **top-level architectural blueprint** designed to break the fundamental deadlock that end-to-end (E2E) and deep-learning black-box perception systems face in safety auditing and compliance.

As a practitioner in arts and humanities, my long-term work revolves around observing the physical world through the lenses of spatiotemporal mapping and medium boundaries. I have noticed a persistent dilemma in the autonomous vehicle (AV) industry: when confronted with the inherent physical limitations of hardware—such as 1% radar noise, multi-path reflections, and ghost anomalies—engineers inevitably default to an algorithmic trap: *using a more complex black box (Deep Neural Networks) to mask the flaws of another hardware black box.* This makes the system virtually impossible to audit under strict functional safety standards like ISO 26262.

To break this loop, I have introduced a zero-invasive, ultra-low-compute **Bypass Binary Space Mask Architecture** rooted in the **Time-Mapping-Framework (TMF)**. By utilizing the *Spatial-Medium Radius Abrupt Mutation Method*, this framework isolates the **99% deterministic physical scenarios** as a fully auditable white-box, while safely routing the remaining 1% of chaotic anomalies back to the pre-existing black-box system as a fallback.

This RFC formally calls upon the global embodied AI and autonomous driving open-source communities—with a warm invitation to scholars, researchers, and entrepreneurial alumni from my alma mater, Peking University—to collaborate on the engineering implementation, core algorithmic development, and joint commercialization of this framework.

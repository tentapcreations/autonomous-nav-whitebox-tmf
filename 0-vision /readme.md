Hey, make some room in your mind.

We need space for a fundamentally different way of letting robots roam.

The cognitive ability to truly understand structure, deconstruct obstacles, and isolate fluid variables is deeply rooted in art and Deconstructivism. Not science. it is a historical fact that engineers cannot refute. Just look at Leonardo da Vinci, who mapped spatial mediums five centuries before Einstein finally awakened science to dynamic spacetime.

Right now, the robotics and autonomous driving industry is suffocating under data-hungry, black-box neural networks — systems that are fuzzy, statistical, and probabilistic, rather than aligned with the beautifully clear, solid, and deterministic physical world. 

**TMF (Traversable Medium Framework)** proposes a radically different foundation. It reframes the world not as a collection of discrete obstacles, but as a **traversable medium** defined by fundamental physical properties. No more headaches over edge-case obstacles, endless object categories, or pedestrian dart-out scenarios.  

In this world model, robots speak in 1s and 0s — not the probabilistic, fuzzy language of large models, but a clean, deterministic language grounded in absolute certainty, structural confidence, and mathematical transparency.

The core idea has been reviewed and recognized by a professor with a dual background in theoretical physics and computer science. Its author graduated from Peking University with a background in Art Theory, and is now calling for open-minded engineers to explore this new way of seeing space.

Stop focusing on obstacles — and you will encounter none of them on the way!
